Smartphones Become Babysitters

Dec 30, 2025 · 2 min read

What Was Happening?

Children aged 0–10 possess boundless energy and require constant supervision for safety. Parents juggling work and childcare responsibilities often resort to handing over their smartphones to keep kids occupied.

Across multiple scenarios — nephews, train passengers, airplane travelers — young users are completely engulfed in endlessly scrolling through YouTube or Instagram Shorts, or playing games.

The content consumed proves problematic. Children absorb material their developing minds cannot properly process. This behavior contributes to shrinking attention spans and creates dependency on short-term dopamine rewards. When these stimulations cease, children experience agitation, anger, or sadness.

Cheaper internet availability in India has accelerated this trend, pulling children into constant scrolling from early ages. While parents remain unaware of long-term consequences, they recognize the immediate benefit: a moment of peace to relax.

So, What's the Business Potential Here?

A significant opportunity exists for an application that transforms doomscrolling into an engaging educational experience. Such a platform could enable children to brainstorm, explore narratives, and learn science through compelling formats.

While competing against AI slop filled with high-contrast graphics presents challenges, this represents an underexplored market opportunity deserving exploration.
