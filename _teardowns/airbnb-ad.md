I was recently looking at different Airbnb ads and decided to write a teardown on what I think of it.

**Video:** If you want to get closer to nature, you might need to get away from hotels. Get an Airbnb and stay in the middle of nowhere.

## Why this ad?

1. Positioning consumers to think about Airbnb when they want to be in nature or somewhere remote
2. Reactivating users to come back on platform — form habit, re-engage
3. Programming when *not* to use a hotel. Sell a good time in a remote place that a hotel can't offer
4. Selling culture, nature, and authentic experience
5. Targeting young adults — couples, friend groups

**What this ad doesn't sell:** Airbnb's platform, features, or booking experience. It focuses on reactivating users who already know what Airbnb offers.

## What's the problem with this ad?

1. Most hotels are listed on both Booking.com and Airbnb, so the "unique escape" positioning has a leak — the same property is findable elsewhere.
2. Booking.com and Expedia can discount loyal customers on the same Airbnb properties, undercutting the value.

## Breaking it down

### What is Airbnb?

- Air Bed and Breakfast — lets people stay in other people's homes and hotels
- Situated in cities and remote places
- Hosts rent out their room or apartment for cash, or for social reasons (meeting people, company)
- Consumers book via app or website, pay upfront, sometimes turn to Airbnb when hotels are booked or too expensive

### Competitors

- **Own the asset:** Hotels, motels, homestays, hostels
- **Search / aggregators:** Booking.com, Expedia

### How does Airbnb make money?

- **Consumer side:** Service fees, cleaning fees, cut of experiences
- **Host side:** Membership, consultation programs

### The flywheel

More consumers → more bookings → more revenue → incentive for more diverse supply → more interest in stays → more consumers.

### What does Airbnb actually sell?

- A place to stay away from crowds and busy city centres
- Authentic culture of a city, town, or area
- Home away from home — usually an entire place, not a hotel room
- Identity signal: the off-the-beaten-path traveler, not a generic tourist

## Jobs to be Done

1. Want to be in nature, solitude, connect with self
2. Away from people and noise
3. Reconnect with a partner, heal
4. Authentic experiences over packaged tourism
5. Signal a certain identity: the curious, authentic traveler
