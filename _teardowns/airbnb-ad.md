I was recently looking at different AirBnB's ads and decided to write a teardown on what I think of it. 

Video Title:  If you want to get closer to nature, you might need to get away from hotels. Get an Airbnb and stay in the middle of nowhere.


Why this ad? 
1. Positioning consumers to think about AirBnB when they want to be in nature/remote
2. Reactivating users to come back on platform --> form habit/engage
3. Programming when to not use a hotel. Sell a good time/vibe in remote place which you cannot attain in a hotel
4. Selling culture/nature/authentic experience
5. Targeting young adults (couples, friends)

What does this ad not sell? AirBnB's platform, feature, experience. Ads focus on reactivating users who already already know what AirBnB offers. 

What's the problem with this ad? 
1. Most hotels/place to stay are on booking.com and on AirBnB to make sure their room allocation is 100%. This takes away the uniqueness.
2. Places like booking.com, Expedia can discount it's loyal customers discount on same AirBnB properties.

Let's break it down
1. What is AirBnB? What are it's characteristics? 
	1. Air Bed and Breakfast
	2. Lets people stay in other people’s home and hotels
	3. Situated in cities and remote place
	4. Hosts/People rent out their room or apartment for:
		1. Cash?
		2. Social - Meeting new people? Older people who like company, young people who like the cash inflow and people to talk to. 
	5. Consumers?
		1. Use app or website to book rooms
		2. Trust to book a room online and pay up front 
		3. Sometimes look at AirBnB when hotels are all booked or expensive
2. Competitors
	1. Own the property/Asset
		1. Hotels
		2. Motels 
		3. Home-stays
		4. Hostels 
	2. Search Business 
		1. Booking 
		2. Expedia
3. How does AirBnB make money? 
	1. Consumers 
		1. Renting rooms? Fees? Service, Cleaning, etc
		2. Cut of experiences
	2. Host buying AirBnB membership/consultation 
4. Fly-Wheel?
	1. More Consumers(Demand) --> More Booking(More revenue) --> Incentive for Supply & More Diverse Supply --> More Interest in stays --> More Consumers 	
5. What does AirBnB sell? 
	1. A place to stay away from crowd, somewhere away from busy centers 
	2. They sell authentic culture of a city, town, area. 
	3. Customer can live in the real part of city and feel the new culture, nature, etc 
	4. They rent a place (usually an entire house) so people can feel like they’re in home = Home away from home
6. JTBD 
	1. Want to be in nature, solitude, connect with self 
	2. Away from people
	3. Reconnect with partner, heal 
	4. Authentic experiences
	5. Signal a certain identity: the authentic, off the beaten path traveler, not a generic tourist
