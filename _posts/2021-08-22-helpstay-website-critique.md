HelpStay.com offers free volunteering abroad opportunities in over 100 countries — you swap skills for accommodation. The concept is compelling. The website creates friction at every step.

## Landing Page

The newsletter subscription banner is the first thing you see. Before a visitor understands what HelpStay is or whether they want it, they're being asked for their email address. That's local optimization hurting global conversion.

On mobile, this banner dominates the entire screen, pushing the actual value proposition below the fold. You're sacrificing the pitch to capture emails from people who don't yet know what they're signing up for.

The $20 annual signup fee is disclosed late in the onboarding flow. Users who reach that point having already invested time feel baited.

## Trust Signals Are Missing

The "Journal" section features paid blogs, not testimonials from actual volunteers. For a product where you're asking people to travel internationally and live with strangers, social proof is everything. Reddit communities and Facebook groups exist for this exact reason — integrate them rather than competing with them.

Stay listings have no ratings or reviews. There's no way to know if a host is reliable. Heart or bookmark counts could at least show which opportunities are popular, even without full reviews.

## User Anxieties Not Addressed

A prospective volunteer has real questions: Is this safe? What's COVID policy? What's security like for solo women travelers? What if something goes wrong? The website doesn't acknowledge any of these concerns. Leading with featured testimonials from volunteers who've had those exact questions would change the dynamic entirely.
